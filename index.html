Engineering Mechanics Notes

Basic Concepts for Mechanical and Civil Engineering

1. Force

Force is defined as a push or pull acting on a body that can change its state of rest or uniform motion.

F = m × a

Where:
F = Force (Newton)
m = Mass (kg)
a = Acceleration (m/s²)

Example: If a body of mass 5 kg accelerates at 2 m/s²,
Force = 5 × 2 = 10 N

2. Moment of Force

Moment is the rotational effect of a force about a point or axis. It depends on the magnitude of force and the perpendicular distance from the point of rotation.

Moment (M) = Force × Distance

Unit: Newton-meter (N·m)

3. Equilibrium

A body is said to be in equilibrium when the net force and net moment acting on the body are zero.

ΣF = 0 ΣM = 0

These two conditions ensure that the body remains in a stable state without translation or rotation.

4. Friction

Friction is the resisting force that opposes relative motion between two surfaces in contact.

F = μ × N

Where:
μ = Coefficient of friction
N = Normal reaction

5. Types of Mechanics

  • Statics – Study of bodies at rest.
  • Dynamics – Study of bodies in motion.
  • Kinematics – Study of motion without considering forces.
  • Kinetics – Study of motion considering forces.
